This network meta-analysis compared neoadjuvant regimens for early and locally advanced triple-negative breast cancer using phase 2-3 randomized controlled trials from Medline, Embase, and the Cochrane Library, identified through August 2025. Eligible regimens included standard chemotherapy, PD-1 and PD-L1 inhibitors, and PARP inhibitors.

Maximum tumor response — The combination of a PD-1 inhibitor with platinum and dose-dense anthracycline–taxane chemotherapy was associated with the highest pathologic complete response rate, but at the cost of greater hematologic toxicity (notably anemia), requiring intensive monitoring and supportive care.

Best survival outcomes — Adding a PD-L1 inhibitor to dose-dense anthracycline–taxane chemotherapy was associated with the greatest improvements in overall survival as well as event-free, disease-free, and relapse-free survival.

Favorable efficacy–safety balance — A PD-1 inhibitor combined with anthracycline–taxane chemotherapy, and a PD-L1 inhibitor combined with a PARP inhibitor and anthracycline–taxane chemotherapy, may offer good efficacy while keeping toxicity more manageable.

Safety signals — Dose-dense anthracycline–taxane chemotherapy increased the risk of grade 3-4 neutropenia and thrombocytopenia, an important consideration when intensifying treatment.

Clinical takeaway — These findings support a more individualized approach to neoadjuvant treatment selection in TNBC, weighing each patient's potential for benefit against the expected toxicity of intensified regimens.
